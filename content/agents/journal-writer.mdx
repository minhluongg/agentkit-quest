The `journal-writer` is a [subagent](/guides/skills-vs-commands-vs-subagents) that documents
significant moments in a project's development — a bug that took a day, an approach that
failed, an architectural decision and why it was made.

## What it does

Captures technical journal entries for the events worth remembering: repeated test failures,
critical bugs, failed approaches that required a rethink, security issues, and the reasoning
behind hard decisions. It's a decision log and a lessons-learned record.

## Honest framing: this is a taste, not a necessity

This is the most optional of AgentKit's agents. A decision journal is genuinely valuable on
long-lived team projects — six months later, "why did we do it this way?" has an answer.
But plenty of developers never keep one and don't miss it. If you don't already write
decision records, an agent that writes them for you is a nice-to-have, not a reason to buy
anything.

## Why it's a subagent

Writing a good journal entry means reviewing what happened — the diffs, the failed attempts,
the conversation — and distilling it. That's a read-and-summarize job whose output (the
entry) is what you keep.

## Build your own (free)

```md
---
name: journal-writer
description: Write a technical journal entry about a significant event. Delegate record-keeping here.
tools: Read, Grep, Write
model: haiku
---

Write a concise journal entry about the significant technical event: what happened, why it
mattered, the decision made and the alternatives rejected, and the lesson. Be honest,
including about what went wrong. No filler.
```

## When it earns its place

- Long-lived team projects where "why did we decide this?" will be asked later.
- If you already value decision records; skip it if you don't.
