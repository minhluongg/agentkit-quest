The `brainstormer` is a [subagent](/guides/skills-vs-commands-vs-subagents) for the step
*before* planning — exploring solution options, debating architectural approaches, and
weighing trade-offs while the decision is still open.

## What it does

Evaluates multiple approaches to a problem — WebSockets vs SSE vs polling, REST vs GraphQL,
build vs buy — with honest trade-off analysis rather than jumping to the first idea. It's
the thinking partner for "which way should we go," feeding into the
[planner](/agents/planner) once a direction is chosen.

## Why it's a subagent

Exploring options generates a lot of "what if" reasoning you don't need cluttering your
main context once you've decided. And a separate agent, told to argue *against* each option,
surfaces trade-offs a single enthusiastic thread glosses over.

## Build your own (free)

```md
---
name: brainstormer
description: Explore and debate approaches to a problem with trade-off analysis. Delegate ideation here.
tools: Read, Grep, WebSearch
---

Given a problem, propose 2–4 genuinely different approaches. For each: how it works, its
real trade-offs, and when it's the right choice. Argue against your own favorite. End with a
recommendation and why — not a hedge.
```

The "argue against your favorite" instruction is what turns brainstorming from
option-listing into real evaluation.

## When it earns its place

- Genuinely open architectural decisions with several viable paths.
- Before committing to an approach you'll be stuck with.
