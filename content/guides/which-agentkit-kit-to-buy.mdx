## If you write code → Engineer

This is the one to buy. It is the kit built and reviewed against real projects, and the
one whose workflow (plan → implement → review → ship) actually holds together — the
subagents hand off to each other rather than being 17 loose tools. If your job is
shipping software, Engineer is the whole answer. You do not need Marketing.

## If you grow a product → Marketing

If your day is lead gen, SEO, email, and content rather than code, Marketing is the
matching kit: 28 agents aimed at research, outreach, and content, with MCP integrations
into the tools that work lives in. A developer does not need this, and a growth marketer
does not need Engineer.

## When the Bundle is — and isn't — worth it

The Bundle saves $49 over buying both. That $49 is only a saving **if you would have
bought both anyway.** If you buy the Bundle to get a kit you will not open, you did not
save $49 — you spent an extra $50 on shelfware.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Buy the Bundle if…
    - You are a solo founder or a tiny team who genuinely both **ships the product and
      does its marketing** — the classic indie-hacker case.
    - You already know you want both kits. Then the $49 is a real discount.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Don't buy the Bundle if…
    - You are a developer with a marketing person (or vice versa). Buy your kit; let them
      buy theirs.
    - You are buying "to have it just in case." An unused kit is not insurance, it is $50.
    - You are not sure. Buy the one kit you need now — it is a one-time purchase, and you
      can add the other later if the need turns out to be real.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## A word on the Desktop app deal

AgentKit is also pre-selling a **Desktop app**: $19/year alone, or **$99/year for the app
plus both kits** (first 100 customers, marketed as a $297 value). Two things to weigh
honestly:

- It is **annual**, not one-time. The kits on their own are a single payment; the app
  bundle is a subscription. Over three years, $99/yr is ~$297 versus a one-time $149
  Bundle. You are paying for the app and ongoing updates, not just the kits.
- It is a **waitlist / pre-sale**, so you are buying partly on a roadmap. If you want the
  kits *now* and are unsure about a desktop control-centre, the one-time Bundle is the
  lower-risk buy.

If a GUI control-centre for your kits genuinely appeals and you'll use it yearly, the
app-plus-kits deal is priced aggressively. If you just want the skills and agents, buy
the kit and skip the subscription.
